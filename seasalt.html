When Japan first opened ports for international trade, they noticed that there was a high demand for green tea exports. After using machines to speed up the process of preparing green tea, they realised that the waste produced in the process increased and wanted a solution to minimise wastage.

In the 1920s, a tea merchant in Kyoto decided to experiment different ways to fix the problem. One of the ways was by roasting leftover green tea leaves, stalks, stems and twigs over charchoal. This process turned out to be successful, and soon after, the term "Hojicha" was coined.
